About

Systems over staff.

Xcede exists because we saw an entire industry doing it wrong — hiring more people to do more busywork instead of building systems that compound.

The Problem We Solve

The headcount trap.

Most B2B companies grow marketing the same way they grow everything else: add headcount. Need more content? Hire a writer. Need better campaigns? Hire an agency. Need attribution? Hire an analyst. The result is linear scaling at best — 2x the people rarely delivers 2x the output, and almost never delivers 2x the insight.

What it does deliver is coordination overhead, communication bottlenecks, and fragile dependencies on individuals. One person leaves and an entire capability vanishes. One agency contract ends and the pipeline dries up. Nothing was built to last because nobody was building systems — they were filling seats.

AI changes the equation, but only if you rethink the architecture. Bolting AI tools onto broken processes just gives you faster busywork. The opportunity is to build marketing operations that compound — where every investment in automation, intelligence, and infrastructure makes the next initiative cheaper, faster, and more effective than the last.

Our Philosophy

The Founder

Built by a practitioner.

Xcede was founded by Duncan Cumming — a marketing operator, not a career consultant. With a cross-border UK-US perspective honed across dozens of B2B engagements, Duncan built the Xcede methodology from the patterns he kept seeing in real client work: brilliant teams trapped in manual processes, expensive tools gathering dust, and marketing strategies that existed only as slide decks.

His background spans marketing automation, systems architecture, and revenue operations — the intersection where strategy actually meets execution. Before Xcede, he spent years building and running the marketing engines that most consultancies only advise on, giving him an operator's instinct for what scales and what breaks.

The methodology isn't theory. It's the codification of what works — refined through every engagement, every system build, every hard-won lesson about what happens when you stop hiring for activity and start engineering for leverage.
