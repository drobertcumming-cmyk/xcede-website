All 19 Pillars

Every dimension of your marketing operation.

Each pillar is scored independently during the diagnostic. Together they reveal the complete picture of your marketing maturity — and the precise sequence for improvement.

Domain 1 — Intelligence & Strategy

4 Pillars
01

Market Research & Intelligence

The foundation of every strategic decision. This pillar evaluates how systematically your organisation gathers, synthesises, and acts on market data — including competitor movements, industry trends, and shifts in buyer behaviour. Without continuous intelligence, marketing strategy becomes guesswork.

Leverage Point Companies with mature market intelligence capabilities make faster, more accurate strategic bets — reducing wasted spend on campaigns that miss the market.
02

ICP & Segmentation

Measures the precision of your ideal customer profiles and the sophistication of your market segmentation. This pillar assesses whether your organisation truly understands who its most valuable buyers are, what distinguishes high-value segments from low-value ones, and how well that understanding informs targeting across every channel.

Leverage Point Precise segmentation is the single greatest multiplier on marketing efficiency — it determines whether your budget reaches the people most likely to buy.
03

Buyer Psychology & Emotional Trigger Mapping

Evaluates how deeply your organisation understands the emotional and psychological drivers behind purchase decisions. This goes beyond demographics and firmographics into the fears, aspirations, objections, and decision-making patterns that actually govern whether a prospect moves forward or stalls.

Leverage Point When messaging aligns with genuine buyer psychology rather than assumed needs, conversion rates increase at every stage of the funnel without increasing spend.
04

Brand Positioning & Competitive Narrative

Assesses the clarity, differentiation, and consistency of your brand's market position. This pillar examines whether your organisation has a defensible narrative that separates it from competitors — and whether that narrative is consistently expressed across every touchpoint, from the website to sales conversations.

Leverage Point Strong positioning reduces acquisition costs by giving prospects a clear reason to choose you — eliminating the need to compete on price alone.

Domain 2 — Messaging & Conversion

3 Pillars
05

Messaging & Offer Architecture

Evaluates the structure and effectiveness of your core messaging framework and the design of your offers. This pillar examines whether your value propositions are clear, whether your messaging adapts to different audience segments and funnel stages, and whether your offers create genuine urgency and perceived value.

Leverage Point Messaging architecture determines how much of your traffic converts — weak messaging makes even high-quality leads abandon the funnel.
06

Funnel Architecture & Conversion Pathways

Measures the intentionality and effectiveness of your end-to-end conversion pathways. This pillar assesses whether your marketing function has clearly defined stages from awareness through to purchase, whether each transition point is deliberately designed, and whether there are systematic mechanisms to recover prospects who disengage.

Leverage Point Most companies lose the majority of potential revenue between funnel stages — structured conversion pathways recover that leakage systematically.
07

CRO & Experimentation

Assesses your organisation's capacity for continuous, data-driven optimisation of conversion performance. This pillar evaluates whether testing is systematic or ad-hoc, whether there is a disciplined experimentation process in place, and whether insights from tests are documented and applied across the marketing operation.

Leverage Point A mature experimentation discipline compounds small gains into significant revenue improvements over time — without requiring additional traffic or budget.

Domain 3 — Channels & Distribution

6 Pillars
08

Email Lifecycle & Automation

Evaluates the sophistication and performance of your email marketing infrastructure. This pillar examines whether email is treated as a strategic lifecycle channel with behaviour-triggered sequences, or merely as a broadcast tool. It assesses deliverability health, segmentation depth, and the degree to which email drives measurable revenue outcomes.

Leverage Point Email remains the highest-ROI channel in B2B marketing — but only when it operates as an automated system, not a manual send schedule.
09

SMS & Omnichannel Orchestration

Measures your ability to coordinate messaging across multiple channels in a unified, coherent experience. This pillar assesses whether SMS, push notifications, and other direct channels are integrated into your broader marketing strategy, or operating as disconnected silos with overlapping or contradictory messages.

Leverage Point Buyers interact across multiple channels before purchasing — disjointed experiences erode trust and reduce conversion at every touchpoint.
10

Paid Traffic & Media Strategy

Evaluates the strategic sophistication and efficiency of your paid acquisition channels. This pillar examines whether paid media is being used with clear unit economics, whether targeting leverages your ICP data, and whether there is a systematic approach to testing creative, audiences, and placements rather than relying on platform defaults.

Leverage Point Paid media without strategic discipline is the fastest way to burn budget — structured media operations consistently reduce cost-per-acquisition over time.
11

Content Strategy & Demand Generation

Assesses whether your content operates as a strategic demand-generation engine or merely fills a publishing calendar. This pillar examines the alignment between content topics and buyer intent, the effectiveness of content distribution, and whether content assets actively generate and nurture pipeline rather than simply existing for brand presence.

Leverage Point Strategic content creates compounding inbound demand over time — reducing dependence on paid channels and building durable competitive advantage.
12

Social Media & Community

Evaluates your organisation's social presence and community-building capability as a strategic marketing function. This pillar measures whether social channels contribute to pipeline and brand equity, whether there is genuine engagement with relevant audiences, and whether community assets are being developed as long-term competitive moats.

Leverage Point Social and community channels build trust at scale before prospects ever enter the funnel — shortening sales cycles and reducing acquisition cost.
13

Influencer, Affiliate & Partnership Strategy

Measures how effectively your organisation leverages third-party relationships to extend reach and credibility. This pillar assesses whether partnerships are pursued strategically with clear selection criteria and performance expectations, or opportunistically without measurable outcomes.

Leverage Point Third-party endorsement and distribution can dramatically accelerate market penetration — but only when partnerships are structured with clear mutual value.

Domain 4 — Infrastructure & Operations

5 Pillars
14

Lead Generation & Magnet Strategy

Assesses the quality, diversity, and conversion effectiveness of your lead generation mechanisms. This pillar evaluates whether your organisation has a systematic approach to attracting qualified prospects through compelling lead magnets, landing pages, and capture mechanisms — and whether those leads are genuinely qualified or merely filling a database.

Leverage Point The quality of leads entering your system determines the efficiency of everything downstream — poor lead generation poisons the entire funnel.
15

Automation, CRM & Workflow Engineering

Evaluates the maturity of your marketing technology infrastructure and the degree to which manual processes have been replaced by reliable automated workflows. This pillar examines CRM data hygiene, integration between systems, and whether your automation actually reduces operational burden or has become its own source of complexity.

Leverage Point Automation is what separates marketing operations that scale from those that collapse under their own weight — it is the backbone of compounding growth.
16

Personalisation & Adaptive Journeys

Measures your capability to deliver dynamically personalised experiences based on real-time behaviour, preferences, and lifecycle stage. This pillar assesses whether personalisation goes beyond inserting a first name — into genuinely adaptive content, offers, and journey paths that respond to individual prospect behaviour.

Leverage Point True personalisation increases engagement and conversion rates across every channel — making the same audience significantly more valuable without growing it.
17

Attribution & Measurement

Assesses your ability to accurately understand what is driving revenue and what is consuming budget without return. This pillar evaluates whether your organisation has reliable attribution across the full customer journey, whether reporting drives actual decisions, and whether measurement sophistication matches the complexity of your marketing operation.

Leverage Point Without accurate attribution, every budget allocation is a guess — mature measurement transforms marketing from a cost centre into a predictable growth engine.
18

Compliance & Risk Management

Evaluates how well your marketing operations manage regulatory, legal, and reputational risk. This pillar examines data privacy compliance, advertising standards adherence, consent management, and whether your organisation has proactive risk governance or is simply hoping problems do not arise.

Leverage Point Compliance failures destroy trust instantly and can halt operations entirely — proactive risk management protects the value of every other pillar.

Domain 5 — Retention & Growth

1 Pillar
19

Retention, LTV & Expansion

Measures your organisation's capability to retain customers, maximise lifetime value, and systematically expand revenue from existing accounts. This pillar assesses whether retention is treated as a strategic priority with dedicated systems and measurement, or as an afterthought once the initial sale is complete. It examines onboarding effectiveness, engagement monitoring, churn prediction, and expansion revenue mechanisms.

Leverage Point Acquiring a new customer costs five to seven times more than retaining one — yet most B2B companies invest disproportionately in acquisition while ignoring the highest-margin growth sitting inside their existing customer base.
How It Works

Three steps from diagnosis to transformation.

The M.O.S. is not a theoretical model. It is an operational diagnostic that produces a scored, prioritised action plan.

1

Score

Every pillar is rated 0–100 during the Tier 1 diagnostic. The result is a complete maturity map of your marketing operation — revealing strengths, weaknesses, and the gaps you did not know existed.

2

Prioritise

Not all pillars matter equally for your business. The highest-leverage interventions are identified and sequenced by expected impact — so you build the right things in the right order.

3

Build

Systems are deployed pillar by pillar through Tiers 2–4 of the engagement. Each build phase delivers standalone value and compounds onto previous work — creating permanent operational infrastructure.
